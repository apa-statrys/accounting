## Background

In Block 1 (Sales Invoice) of the Statrys accounting feature, clients need to raise sales invoices
directly in the mobile app, send them to their customers, and have them resolve to Paid once the
money arrives — connecting the accounting side to the payment side.

**Comments — For Recurring Sales Invoice Generation option — see Story 48.**

Clients who bill the same customers on a regular basis — retainers, subscriptions, monthly
services — need a way to generate invoices automatically on a fixed schedule without recreating
them each time.

## Goal

Let a client create, issue, send, and resolve a sales invoice through its full status lifecycle
(Draft → Awaiting Payment → Overdue / Partially Paid / Paid / Void) from the mobile app. This story
covers the creation lifecycle of the invoice. The additional fields of the invoice are detailed in
DES-764.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Invoice is automatically saved as a draft**
   GIVEN a client selects "Create Invoice" from the invoice list
   THEN the system immediately creates an invoice in **Draft** status
   AND assigns an Invoice Type, Invoice Source, Invoice Status, before the form is filled or sent
   AND displays the invoice form pre-filled
   AND the invoice is saved automatically — **no manual save action is required**

2. **Client completes and sends the invoice**
   GIVEN a draft invoice with all required fields completed
   WHEN the client selects "Send"
   THEN the system validates the required fields
   AND if validation passes,
   THEN the system emails the invoice as a PDF to the customer's stored email address and/or
   generates a shareable link
   AND moves the invoice status from Draft to **Awaiting Payment**
   AND the invoice number is system-generated and **cannot be changed after this point**

3. **Invoice cannot be edited or deleted after Paid status**
   GIVEN an invoice in Paid status
   THEN the invoice is non-editable and non-deletable
   AND no action is available on it except viewing

4. **Cancel an issued invoice with a credit note**
   GIVEN an invoice in **Awaiting Payment** status
   WHEN the client selects "Cancel with Credit Note" and completes the Credit Note flow
   THEN the invoice moves to **Void** and is non-editable

5. **Client deletes a draft invoice**
   GIVEN an invoice in Draft status
   WHEN the client selects "Delete"
   THEN the system displays a **confirmation prompt** before proceeding
   AND on confirmation, permanently removes the invoice from the system
   AND deletion is only available in Draft — no other status permits deletion

### Other additional path implications [to link with Block 3 stories]

6. **Accountant marks invoice as paid — manual path** *(out of scope for this story)*
   GIVEN an invoice in Awaiting Payment status
   WHEN the accountant selects "Mark as paid" on the back office
   THEN the invoice status moves to Paid
   AND the client sees the invoice as Paid in the mobile app

7. **Accountant marks invoice as paid — reconciliation path** *(out of scope for this story)*
   GIVEN an invoice in Awaiting Payment status
   AND a matching bank transaction has been identified in the reconciliation queue
   WHEN the accountant validates the reconciliation match on the back office
   THEN the invoice status updates based on the matched amount per the Reconciliation Outcomes table
   AND a fully matched invoice moves to Paid and becomes non-editable
   AND a partially matched invoice moves to Partially Paid with the remaining outstanding balance displayed

## Rules

**Invoice Status Matrix**

| Status | Editable? | Entered When | Deletable? | Terminal? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Draft | Yes | On create, before issuing; when closing the invoice screen | Yes | No |
| Awaiting Payment | Limited (only Due Date & Automated reminder) | Invoice issued | No | No |
| Overdue | Limited (only Due Date & Automated reminder) | Awaiting Payment and due date has passed, unpaid (client is notified) | No | No |
| Partially Paid | No | Reconciliation matches a payment less than the total | No | No |
| Paid | No | Reconciliation matches the full total (or overpayment) | No | Yes |
| Void | No | Cancel with Credit Note completed | No | Yes |
| Pending Refund | No | CN is marked as refunded by clients | No | No |
| Refunded | No | CN is fully refunded and approved by accountant | No | No |

**Due date:** Users can choose between 30, 60, 90 days or select a custom date.

**Overdue:** End of due date based on local timezone.

**Reconciliation Outcomes**

| Matched Amount vs Invoice Total | Resulting Status |
| --- | --- |
| Equal to total | Paid |
| Less than total | Partially Paid (remainder still due) |
| Greater than total | Paid, with the overpayment flagged for review (credit/refund) |

**Send Channels**

| Channel | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Email | PDF sent to the client's stored Email Address (prompt to add one if missing) |
| Shareable link | Link the client can share with the customer; always available |

## Notes

- Surface: created and sent from the **mobile app**; the Operation Manager **back office is web**.
- **Overdue** notifies the Statrys client (the sender) only — they decide whether to chase the
  customer. "Overdue" itself is a time-based transition, not a user action.
- **Editing an issued invoice** is limited and regenerates + re-sends the document; this differs
  from the Edit Client rule, which only propagates client-detail changes to draft invoices.
- **Out of scope:** AC6, AC7 and the overpayment logic (credit note vs refund) are not implemented
  in this story (only status for Sales invoices).
