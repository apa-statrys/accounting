Invoice statuses by entry point — Manual vs Upload

A normal (non-recurring) invoice enters one of two ways: created manually in the app, or uploaded and read by OCR. The two behave differently at the Draft stage only — because an uploaded invoice was already issued outside the app, so you record it rather than send it. Once issued, both follow one identical lifecycle. Docks show the two dock CTAs; ⋯ lists the actions sheet.

Where they differ — the Draft stage

Same grey Draft badge, opposite intent. Manual has no number yet and its primary action is to send; Upload already carries the scanned number and its primary action is to record what happened outside the app.

Once the payout settles, the money-out action drops and only the refund credit note's send/resend remains. Partial refunds stay at Pending Refund with a "Partially Refunded" tag.

The one real difference is the Draft dock. Manual leads with Send invoice (it hasn't gone out yet); Upload leads with Mark as sent / Mark as paid (it already went out — you're logging it). Upload also carries Send invoice in its ⋯ menu as an optional "email a copy."

The spine is shared: Draft → Awaiting Payment → Paid. Overdue is a derived state of Awaiting; Partially Paid, Void, and the two refund states are branches off a sent invoice — identical for both entry points.
